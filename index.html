Drug Harm Awareness

Substance abuse is a growing crisis, especially among youth. In 2020, 40.3 million people in the U.S. suffered from substance use disorders. Over 107,000 deaths were reported in 2021, largely due to fentanyl overdoses.

Every pill can be a trap. Choose life over addiction.

Prevalence & Death Rates

1 in every 7.5 Americans had a substance use disorder in 2020. Drug deaths continue to rise, with fentanyl being the most common cause.

  • 107,000+ deaths in 2021
  • 2.75% of all US deaths caused by overdose
  • Highest overdose rates among Black and Native American/Alaska Native populations

Drug Use, Crime & Suicide

Substance use contributes heavily to crime and mental health crises.

  • 60% of prisoners were using drugs during their crime
  • 64% of homicides linked to drugs
  • 80% of inmates abuse drugs or alcohol
  • 75% of suicides involve substances
Drugs don’t just end lives — they destroy futures.

Physical & Mental Effects

Drug use damages the body and mind severely, especially in younger users.

  • High blood pressure, strokes, seizures
  • Organ failure, memory loss, hallucinations
  • Permanent brain damage, paranoia, mood swings
  • Linked to psychosis and schizophrenia, especially in young males using cannabis
Addiction doesn’t care who you are — it always takes more than it gives.

Recovery & Rehabilitation

Recovery is possible but requires commitment and time.

  • 40–60% relapse after completing rehab
  • Less than 43% complete rehab programs
  • 3+ months of treatment yields the best results
  • Recovery leads to lower criminal behavior and improved life quality

Prevention & Education

Education is the strongest defense against drug abuse.

  • Community and family-based prevention efforts work
  • Education empowers informed decision-making
  • US spends ~$1 billion/year on prevention vs. $29 trillion GDP
Education saves lives. Spread the truth. Stop the harm.
